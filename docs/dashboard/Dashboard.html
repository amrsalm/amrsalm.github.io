Research Question

Is there a significant difference between high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol and low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol levels?

Methodology

The paired t-test was chosen because the two measurements come from the same individuals.

Results
  • Mean Difference: 50.12989
  • t-statistic: 35.58
  • Degrees of Freedom: 4649
  • p-value: 2.2e-16
  • Confidence Interval (95%): 47.36787 to 52.89192
Conclusion

The paired t-test revealed a statistically significant difference between triglyceride (LBXTR) and HDL cholesterol (LBDHDD) levels (t = 35.582, df = 4649, p-value < 2.2e-16). The mean difference was 50.13, with a 95% confidence interval of [47.37, 52.89]. Since the p-value is less than 0.05, we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that there is a significant difference in the mean levels of triglycerides and HDL cholesterol among the participants.

Research Question

Is there a difference in BMI distribution between males and females?

Methodology

A two-sample t-test was conducted to compare mean BMI between genders.

Results
  • t-statistic: -7.8522
  • Degrees of Freedom: 12883
  • p-value: `4.409e-15
  • Confidence Interval (95%): -1.4346631 to `-0.8614791
Conclusion

The Welch Two Sample t-test indicated a statistically significant difference in mean BMI between males and females (t = -7.8522, df = 12,883, p-value = 4.409e-15). The mean BMI for males was 26.07, while for females it was 27.22. The 95% confidence interval for the difference in means is [-1.43, -0.86]. Since the p-value is less than 0.05, we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that there is a significant difference in mean BMI between males and females.
