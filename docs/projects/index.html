Research Projects

Runs of Homozygosity in Pediatric Cancer

This project explores long ROHs as potential indicators of tumor suppressor inactivation using WGS data from 25,000+ pediatric cancer patients. It integrates ROH mapping, population structure analysis, and enrichment testing for known cancer predisposition loci. Initial findings suggest enrichment of ROH events in key genomic regions around important genes TP53 and EGFR

Skills: BCFtools, Snakemake, R (GenomicRanges, data.table), UCSC Genome Browser, bash

Polygenic Risk Prediction of Kidney Stone Disease

Under Dr. Anirban Mondal, I developed a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to predict kidney stone susceptibility from GWAS-derived SNPs. Despite a small cohort (n = 60), the model achieved 75% accuracy and an ROC-AUC of 0.8125, supporting the use of deep learning for genetic risk modeling in complex traits.

This project laid the foundation for exploring polygenic risk scores (PRS) in urologic conditions. Our findings were presented at ACMG 2024 and published as a preprint on arXiv:2412.17559.

Skills: Python (TensorFlow, pandas), GWAS processing, CNN modeling, PRS development, ROC/AUC evaluation

Reframing Kidney Stones as a Systemic Disease

This study used longitudinal EHR data to uncover novel comorbidity patterns in kidney stone patients. We identified a significant link between kidney stone disease (KSD) and neoplastic disorders, prompting a new view of KSD as a systemic condition rather than a purely urological issue.

Our analysis, featured at ACMG 2024 and in a peer-reviewed publication, is available at ScienceDirect (ACMG GIM findings).

Skills: R (tidymodels, data.table), EHR integration, longitudinal modeling, disease network analysis, logistic regression
